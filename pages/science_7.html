Master's Thesis

Iterative Real-Time Calculation of Quantized Phase Holograms Optimized for Spatial Light Modulators

Master's thesis in Applied Physics submitted to Hochschule Koblenz, RheinAhrCampus Remagen in cooperation with HOLOEYE Photonics AG.

Author: Jan-Maximilian Beneke
Primary reviewer: Prof. Dr. Thomas Wilhein
Secondary reviewer: Prof. Dr. Ulrich Hartmann
External reviewer: Dr. Grigory Lazarev
Keywords: Digital Holography, Holographic Projection, CGH, Real-Time IFTA, GPGPU, OpenCL, NVIDIA CUDA, Parallel Computing
Date: January 22nd, 2012

Abstract

The generation of synthetic holograms is widely used throughout many scientific applications and often accomplished by iterative Fourier-transform algorithms (IFTAs). These iterative approaches provide digital holograms of high quality, but are very costly in computation. This thesis aims to implement an IFTA that allows to process the computer generated holograms (CGHs) of arbitrary intensity distributions very rapidly and in real-time speed whenever possible. To achieve the necessary computational speed, the algorithm will be computed on common consumer graphics cards by using either NVIDIA CUDA or OpenCL. Both programming models are intended to provide comparable high computational efficiency without the use of expensive dedicated hardware. A common application for speedily generated holograms is holographic projection. In this publication, a spatial light modulator (SLM) is used to manipulate the plain wavefronts of a laser beam to be able to use the continuous stream of holograms. The used modulator is basically a liquid crystal display that manipulates only the phase of the penetrating light field. Furthermore, this display can be addressed by the same graphics card that is used for the computation. Besides, the best suited settings for IFTA based projections and their influences in the reconstruction quality of the hologram as well as the computational efficiency are discussed.
